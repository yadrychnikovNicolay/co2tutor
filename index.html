The project

CO2Tutor was born as a part of a hackathon. Our mission is to create awareness of the importance of indoor air quality.

We created a connected IoT device to measure indoor air quality using different variables like CO2 and dust. The device shows its status in real time and logs its readings online to a personal dashboard that also dispenses individualised advice on how to improve indoor air quality.

Poor indoor air quality is directly related to the development of illnesses like asthma and allergies, and to brain development issues in young people. CO2Tutor contributes, by influencing young people using its educational device in the environment in which they grow up, to a world where the potential of young brains can be maximised in concrete and practical ways

Our work

What value does the device create for the customer?

The customer has an extra tool in their arsenal to help learning about the importance of indoor air quality.

Through device’s sensors and and the real time visualisation of the data it provides the importance of the different parameters becomes clear and actionable.

It shows both the negative and positive consequences of specific actions. A historical graph shows how the various actions of better management lead into better air quality over time.
